Food & Drink

Kingston has a vast array of cafes, restaurants, pubs and bars to suit every mood. Whether you're looking for a hearty breakfast, quick and tasty post-work eats or pre-theatre dining, we're sure to have something to tickle your tastebuds around every corner.

Tucked away off the Ancient Market Place is Apple Market where you'll find a charming mix of independent cafes and eateries with tables spilling out into a small piazza; great for lunches and brunches. Along the riverside promenade is a string of brasseries, restaurants and bars, all with outdoor seating in a stunning setting overlooking the Thames.

Nearer the station, you'll find a great range of well-known restaurant names alongside a number of unique independents - perfect for grabbing a quick bite on your way home or before a trip to the cinema.

Places To Stay

Like most towns, Kingston has a number of large national chain hotels; ranging from the budget end with Travelodge and Premier Inn through to the more upscale with the Doubletree by Hilton.

If you are looking for a more unique place to rest your head; then why not take a walk across Kingston Bridge, where you'll find a number of more quaint hotels/pubs. Offering a more homely feel, mixed with some good food, drinks and atmosphere.

For those looking to stay more upmarket but within commutable distance; you may be interested in looking along the Thames at Richmond but be warned you will pay the finanical price for laying your head there.

Attractions

Situated in leafy Surrey on the banks of the famous Thames and just a 25 minute train ride from Waterloo, Kingston provides the perfect base to explore London and the surrounding area; a peaceful oasis away from the hustle and bustle of the city centre.

For nature lovers, Bushy Park is famed for its mix of waterways, gardens and grassland, and roaming herds of red and fallow deer. Whilst those interested in some history & art can visit the Coronation Stone and the Out of Order Postboxes.

Popular attractions locally include Kew Gardens, Chessington World of Adventures and Thorpe Park, not to mention Henry VIII's famous Hampton Court Palace which is located just the other side of the river. Why not take a leisurely stroll or bike ride there, and catch the picturesque boat ride back.

For those looking for a bit of entertainment, the Rotunda is a great venue; with an odeon cinema and David Lloyd Leisure centre.
